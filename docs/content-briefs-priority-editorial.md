- `Pillar`
  - coded page

  - broad topic with multiple spokes

  - Sanity `/news` post
  - editorial article supporting a pillar
  - narrower subtopic, entity, problem, comparison, or explainer
- `Solution / Commercial`
  - coded page
  - acquisition-oriented
  - explicit product category, workflow, or use-case page

- excerpt: `ESSNA matters because EU sports nutrition policy does not stay inside legal teams. It creates practical pressure on claims, labels, localized content, and partner-ready product information across markets.`

### AI Summary Draft

`ESSNA is the European Specialist Sports Nutrition Alliance, a trade body representing the sports nutrition industry in European regulatory discussions. For brands selling into Europe, ESSNA matters because policy changes around claims, labeling, nutrient limits, and novel foods create operational pressure on product data, localized copy, labels, and partner materials. The issue is not only compliance. It is whether teams can update market-specific product content quickly and accurately.`

### Key Facts For AI Retrieval

- ESSNA is the European Specialist Sports Nutrition Alliance
- It represents the sports nutrition sector in EU regulatory discussions
- Relevant issues include claims, front-of-pack labeling, vitamin and mineral limits, EU-UK divergence, and novel foods
- For brands, these issues affect labels, localized copy, claims handling, and market-specific content operations

3. The EU issues it is working on

- vitamins and minerals
- EU-UK divergence
- novel foods

4. Why these issues matter operationally
- product records change by market
- claims and warnings may differ
- labels and sell sheets need updates

5. What teams usually underestimate
- they treat market differences as language differences
- they handle exceptions in spreadsheets
- they do not structure content around markets early enough

6. What brands should do next
- track source changes
- structure product information by market
- separate source content from localized and channel-specific outputs
- establish governed asset and partner workflows

7. Soft disclaimer

### Audience

- supplement brands still running core product content in spreadsheets
- teams feeling launch friction

### AI Summary Draft

`Spreadsheets fail for supplement product content operations when teams need to manage variant complexity, approved assets, localized copy, claims-sensitive fields, and partner-specific outputs at the same time. The issue is not only scale. It is that spreadsheets do not preserve structure, governance, and market-aware content handling well enough for live commercial workflows.`

### Key Facts For AI Retrieval

- spreadsheet failure usually starts with exceptions, not raw SKU count
- supplement brands face variant, market, channel, and claims complexity simultaneously
- disconnected spreadsheets increase drift across data, assets, and partner outputs
- the operational cost appears in launch readiness, rework, and inconsistent downstream content

4. Why teams wait too long to change

5. What better looks like
